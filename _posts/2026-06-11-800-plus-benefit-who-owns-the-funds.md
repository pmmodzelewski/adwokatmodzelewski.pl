**The question of who owns the funds received under the child-rearing benefit (800+) may be of considerable importance.**

## Parents or Children?

The first question to consider is whether the funds obtained under the child-rearing benefit belong to the children. The answer must, however, be in the negative — it is accepted that the purpose of this benefit (and of the Act on State Assistance in Child-Rearing as a whole) is precisely to provide assistance to parents. It is therefore clear that the funds paid out under the scheme belong to the parents, not to the children.

## Personal or Matrimonial Property?

Once we have established that the 800+ funds do not belong to the children, we must consider a second question — whether they form part of the parents' matrimonial joint property (assuming, of course, that they are married and have not established a separation of property regime), or whether they form part of the personal property of one of them. Again, the answer is unsurprising — these funds are not an exception to the general rule and, as a matter of principle, they fall into the matrimonial joint estate, provided that such an estate exists.

## What if There is No Joint Estate?

In such circumstances, the funds form part of the property of whichever parent is the named recipient under the benefit award decision. That parent not only receives the funds by bank transfer but is also their sole owner. They ought to apply the funds in accordance with their intended purpose (covering the costs of raising the child); however, they are under no obligation to account for their use — neither to the other parent nor to the State.

## An Alternative Arrangement

The Act provides for one further option — where care of the child is exercised under a shared care arrangement (piecza naprzemienna), the parents may apply jointly for the benefit to be paid to each of them in equal shares.
