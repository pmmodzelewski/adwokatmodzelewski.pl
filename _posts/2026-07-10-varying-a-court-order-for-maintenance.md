**In order to successfully seek a variation of the maintenance amount awarded by a court order,**
**it is necessary to demonstrate a material change in circumstances, whether on the part of the**
**person entitled to maintenance or the person liable to pay it.**

## A Maintenance Order

Although an increasing number of separating couples are able to resolve key issues relating to their children amicably — including parental responsibility, place of residence, contact arrangements, and maintenance — these matters are usually formalised in a legally binding manner, either by way of a court order or a judicially approved settlement agreement. This provides a framework to which the parties may always return in the event of future disagreement, and which is capable of enforcement through the courts or by a bailiff.

## When May a Maintenance Order Be Varied?

It was the legislature's intention that decisions concerning maintenance should be relatively stable. Family disputes are frequently among the most acrimonious, and one can readily envisage a situation in which a party dissatisfied with a final order commences fresh proceedings immediately upon the conclusion of the previous ones, in the hope of obtaining a more favourable outcome.

To prevent such situations, the applicable provisions require that a party seeking to vary a maintenance order — whether by way of an increase or a reduction — must demonstrate that a material change in circumstances has occurred in the intervening period.

## What Constitutes a Material Change in Circumstances?

It is therefore insufficient to show that, having regard to the costs of maintaining a minor child and the financial situation and means of the liable parent, a different monthly maintenance figure might have been expected. It is necessary to demonstrate that the situation — on one side or the other — has undergone a substantial, fundamental change since the date on which the previous order became final. This may arise from the passage of time (older children generally have greater needs, and inflation causes a steady rise in the cost of living), from changes in the child's circumstances (for example, new extra-curricular activities generating additional expense, a change in health, and so forth), or from changes on the part of a parent (loss of employment, or, conversely, a promotion and a significant improvement in financial position).

Any variation in the maintenance amount should, at the same time, be strictly correlated with the change in circumstances that has been established. An argument that the obligation was previously set at too low or too high a level for one reason or another should not, of itself, be sufficient.

## Practical Implications

These considerations must be borne in mind when deciding whether to bring an action for variation of maintenance, regardless of the role one is to play in any potential dispute. A properly prepared statement of claim in this regard should contain not only up-to-date calculations but also arguments substantiating the change in circumstances. A qualified legal representative can always assist in its preparation.
